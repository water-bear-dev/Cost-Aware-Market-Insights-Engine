      Ticker Discovery Formula

      Developed by Joel Greenblatt in "The Little Book That Still Beats the Market", this formula was selected as the engine's primary discovery layer for three key reasons:

      • • Superior Backtested Alpha: Historically, this strategy has delivered 20-30% annual returns, consistently outperforming the S&P 500 over 20+ year periods.
      • • Noise Elimination: By requiring high Return on Capital (ROC), we instantly filter out 90% of global tickers that have poor unit economics or unsustainable business models.
      • • Holistic Valuation: Using Enterprise Value (EV) instead of just Market Cap ensures we account for a company's debt, providing a "truthful" measure of its acquisition price.

      1. Earnings Yield (EY)

      EBIT / Enterprise Value

      Identifies undervalued assets by looking at pre-tax operating profits relative to the total cost of the business (Debt + Equity).

      2. Return on Capital (ROC)

      EBIT / (Fixed Assets + Working Capital)

      Measures how effectively a company generates profit from the capital invested in its core operations.

      Source: Greenblatt, J. (2005). The Little Book That Still Beats the Market.

      05

      Quality Minus Junk (QMJ) Formula

      Based on the methodology by Asness, Frazzini, and Pedersen (2018), "Quality" is defined as assets with high profitability, growth, and safety. This engine implements a robust version using SEC-standardized financial reports.

      1. Profitability (GP/A)

      The cleanest measure of economic performance, less prone to accounting manipulation.

      Gross Profits / Total Assets

      2. Safety (Low Leverage)

      Quality companies maintain manageable debt levels relative to their equity base.

      Total Debt / Stockholders' Equity

      Composite Score Calculation

      We convert each metric into a cross-sectional percentile (0-100) within each reporting quarter.

      QMJ = (z[Prof] + z[Safe]) / 2

      Where z is the standard percentile ranking. Assets in the 90th percentile for Profitability and 80th for Safety receive a QMJ score of 85.

      Value & Momentum (APM 2013)

      • Value: Measured as Earnings Yield (Net Income / Market Cap). High yield indicates deep value.
      • Momentum: 12-month price return, skipping the most recent month to avoid short-term reversals.

      Sources:
      • Asness, C., Frazzini, A., & Pedersen, L. (2013). Quality Minus Junk.
      • Asness, C., Moskowitz, T., & Pedersen, L. (2013). Value and Momentum Everywhere.
